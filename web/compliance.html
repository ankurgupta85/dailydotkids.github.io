BC Licensed Child Care

Built with BC compliance in mind

Licensed daycares in British Columbia must keep detailed records under the Child Care Licensing Regulation. We designed DailyDotKids to support those day-to-day record-keeping workflows — so your team spends less time on paperwork and more time with children.

Attendance & sign-in/out

BC records: daily attendance

Teachers log arrivals and pickups through configurable Check-In and Check-Out categories — with timestamps, notes, and who picked up each child. Admins and teachers can review history in Class Record, filtered by date, student, and category.

Emergency contacts

BC records: emergency contact information

Store emergency contact name, phone, email, and relationship on each child's profile. Parents can keep details current from the Parent app; teachers see contacts on the class roster when they need them.

Allergies & special care

BC records: allergies and special care instructions

Allergies and other care notes live on the student profile and appear on teacher roster cards — so staff have the right context before logging meals, health events, or daily activities.

Health, medication & incidents

BC records: health and medication logs

Centers configure a Health activity category with options such as Symptoms, Medication, Incident, and Routine Check. Each entry is timestamped and tied to the child — a digital supplement to your existing medication and incident procedures, not a replacement for prescribed forms or physician instructions.

Daily program & family communication

BC records: program documentation & parent communication

Meals, naps, photos, moods, diary notes, daily reports, and director bulletins create a clear picture of each child's day — for families in real time and for your team when reviewing past care.

Staff time on site

BC operations: staff supervision & presence

Teachers clock in and out before logging activities — on personal phones or shared classroom tablets. Owners and admins review per-teacher time logs and flagged outlier sessions from the admin dashboard.

Secure, role-based access

BC & privacy: protecting children's information

Invitation-only accounts, owner vs. location-admin permissions, and scoped data access help keep sensitive child and family information visible only to the right people. See our Privacy Policy for how we handle personal information under Canadian privacy law.

Records for review

BC records: available for licensing inspection

Class Record, class diary, daily reports, and admin roster views give you searchable digital records by date and student. Your center remains responsible for retention practices and any records the platform does not store.

On our roadmap

We know licensed centres keep more than daily activity logs. These are the compliance features we are working toward — starting with a secure document vault and expiry reminders:

  • Compliance document vault — upload and organize immunization records, consent forms, staff CRCs, and certificates per child or staff member
  • Expiry tracking & alerts — reminders before CRCs, first aid, or immunizations are due
  • Parent consent workflow — families upload or acknowledge forms; admins see who is still missing paperwork
  • Fire drill & safety logbook — dated facility logs per location for drills and internal safety records
  • Policy library — centre policies in one place, with staff read-and-acknowledge tracking
  • Licensing visit export — printable or downloadable record packs for inspections

Until these ship, centres continue to keep those records in their existing files. Pilot partners help us prioritize what to build first.

What no app can do

Software can organize records and reminders — but it cannot replace the licensee, the law, or the work of running a safe centre. No childcare app — including DailyDotKids — can:

  • Guarantee that your centre is compliant or licensed
  • Take on the licensee's legal duties under BC child care law
  • Conduct physical facility inspections or verify building and playground safety
  • Automatically verify criminal record checks with police or government systems
  • Provide medical, legal, or licensing advice
  • Replace original signed paper where your licensing officer requires it
  • File serious incident reports to BC licensing on your behalf
  • Supervise children or enforce staff-to-child ratios in the classroom

Your team remains responsible for policies, training, ratios, safe premises, and every obligation on your facility licence.

Important: DailyDotKids does not provide legal advice and does not certify that your center is compliant. The licensee is always responsible for meeting all requirements under the Community Care and Assisted Living Act, the Child Care Licensing Regulation, and your facility licence.
